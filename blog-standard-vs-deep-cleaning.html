Standard vs Deep Cleaning: What's the Difference?

6 min read November 2024

Should you book a standard cleaning or deep cleaning? The answer depends on your home's current condition, how long since the last professional clean, and your specific needs. Here's everything you need to know.

What Standard Cleaning Includes

Standard cleaning maintains a clean home. It covers high-traffic areas and visible surfaces:

  • Kitchen: Countertops, stovetop surface, sink, microwave exterior, appliance exteriors
  • Bathrooms: Toilet, shower/tub, sink, mirrors, floor
  • All Rooms: Dusting surfaces, vacuuming carpets, mopping floors, emptying trash
  • Beds: Making beds (changing linens if left out)

What Deep Cleaning Includes

Deep cleaning tackles accumulated dirt, grime, and areas often missed during regular cleaning:

Kitchen (Deep Clean)

  • Inside oven and microwave
  • Degreasing range hood and filter
  • Inside and outside of cabinets
  • Behind and under appliances
  • Cleaning refrigerator exterior and handles

Bathrooms (Deep Clean)

  • Scrubbing tile grout
  • Removing hard water deposits
  • Behind toilets
  • Shower door tracks
  • Exhaust fans

All Rooms (Deep Clean)

  • Baseboards throughout
  • Door frames and tops of doors
  • Light fixtures and ceiling fans
  • Window sills and tracks
  • Inside closets
  • Under furniture

When to Choose Standard Cleaning

Standard cleaning is right for you if:

  • ✓ Your home was deep cleaned in the last 3-6 months
  • ✓ You want regular maintenance (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)
  • ✓ Your home is already relatively clean
  • ✓ You're on a tighter budget
  • ✓ You need a quick refresh before guests

When to Choose Deep Cleaning

Deep cleaning is right for you if:

  • ✓ It's been 6+ months since your last professional clean
  • ✓ You're moving into a new home
  • ✓ You're preparing to sell your home
  • ✓ You've just finished renovations
  • ✓ You have allergies and need a thorough clean
  • ✓ It's spring cleaning time
  • ✓ You're hosting a major event

The Ideal Cleaning Schedule

For most households, we recommend:

  1. Start with a deep clean to establish a baseline
  2. Follow with standard cleaning every 2-4 weeks
  3. Schedule deep cleaning every 3-6 months (quarterly for best results)

This approach keeps your home consistently clean while addressing deep-seated dirt before it becomes a problem.

Can I Start with Standard Cleaning?

If your home hasn't been professionally cleaned before, we generally recommend starting with deep cleaning. Here's why:

  • Standard cleaning maintains cleanliness — it doesn't create it
  • Built-up grime in overlooked areas won't be addressed
  • You won't see the full difference professional cleaning can make

After the initial deep clean, standard cleaning will be more effective at maintaining your home.
